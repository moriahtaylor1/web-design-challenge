  COMPARING ALL PLOTS



Each of these visualizations show how different characteristics of shape and size relate to the diagnosis of tumors as either benign or malignant.


The area, compactness, concavity, and radius of malignant tumors appears to be significantly higher than those of benign tumors. The averages, represented by the dashed lines, are far apart in the plots. There is still some overlap between the distributions of the groups in each of these categories, so that indicates that within some intervals, it is possible for a tumor to be either benign or malignant.


Symmetry and texture, on the other hand, do not seem to be inherently tied to a tumor's diagnosis. The distributions for each group for both of these characteristics have considerable overlap and their means are comparable. This indicates that symmetry and texture may not be different between benign and malignant tumors.
