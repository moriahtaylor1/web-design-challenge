  AREA


The density plot on the left shows which values of AREA are most common for each group of tumors: benign and malignant. The dashed lines represent the average for each group.


As you can see in the graph, the average area of a malignant tumor is significantly larger than the average area of a benign tumor. The benign tumors in the dataset had an average of approximately 450 while the malignant tumors averaged around 1000.


You can also see that there is overlap between 500 and 750 - this means that a tumor with an area between these two numbers could be equally likely to be benign or malignant.
